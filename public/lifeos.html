The 5-Step Weekly Review Protocol
Step 1 — 5 min
Clear Inboxes
Email, notes, messages to zero. Every capture point emptied. GTD principle: mind like water.
Step 2 — 10 min
Review Metrics
Check this scorecard. Look for trends, not single data points. One week is noise; patterns are signal.
Step 3 — 15 min
Review Goals / OKRs
Are you on track? What slipped? Adjust next week's priorities to compensate. Check Goals tab.
Step 4 — 10 min
Plan Next Week
Time-block deep work sessions first. Research: scheduling with specific time + place doubles follow-through (Gollwitzer 1999, implementation intentions).
Step 5 — 5 min
Gratitude + Shutdown
3 things that went well. Research: gratitude journaling raises wellbeing scores 10–15% at 8 weeks (Emmons & McCullough 2003).

OKR Check-in
Confidence Levels
Google's OKR standard: 60–70% completion = calibrated. 100% = too easy. Use confidence scoring (0–1.0) not just done/not done.
System Design
Change Inputs Not Willpower
BJ Fogg (Tiny Habits): redesign your environment. Willpower is finite (ego depletion, Baumeister 1998). Reduce friction for good habits; increase friction for bad ones.

Mental Models for Decisions
Second-Order Thinking
Ask "and then what?" twice. Most people stop at first-order consequences. (Howard Marks)
Inversion
Instead of "how do I succeed?", ask "what would guarantee failure?" Avoid that. (Charlie Munger)
Regret Minimization
Project to age 80. Which decision will you regret not taking? (Jeff Bezos)
10/10/10 Rule
How will I feel in 10 min, 10 months, 10 years? Counters short-term emotional bias. (Suzy Welch)
Decision journal reduces hindsight bias by forcing you to record reasoning before you know the outcome. Review quarterly.

Tools Arsenal
